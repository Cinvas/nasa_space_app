The Habitable Worlds Observatory is a large infrared, optical, and ultraviolet space telescope concept. It would be the first space telescope specifically created to look for indications of habitability and life on planets around other stars. Discovering life would represent a groundbreaking accomplishment worldwide. This "super-Hubble" would determine if Earth-like planets are common or uncommon. Its large mirror and ability to observe across ultraviolet to infrared wavelengths would offer significant capabilities for groundbreaking astrophysical research.

Now in the shoes of an astronomer you are given the task of searching the Cosmos for exoplanets suitable for life.

Suggested info and where to search for answers

Every star has a habitable zone (region around a star where planets can receive the perfect amount of heat), but where that zone lies is different for stars of different sizes and brightness. When searching for possibly habitable exoplanets, it helps to start with worlds like our own. Whether a body is in the habitable zone of its host star depends on the radius of the planet's orbit and the mass of the body itself. Although there is room for discussions. NASA Image Article If an exoplanet is in the 'habitable zone'—where conditions may allow for liquid water to exist—it has a better chance of supporting life as we know it. It’s important that the planets radius is approximately less than 2.5 of Earth radii, because it shows that the body might have a solid surface. PHL Research
